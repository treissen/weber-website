Weber MAD Operations

Pesticide Applications

The first thing that comes to mind when people think of a Mosquito Abatment is the truck that drives around at night.

While this is a big part of our operation, most of our activities occur during the day. Every week each know mosquito source is checked by one of our technicians, and if larvae are found a treatment plan will be formulated and executed. Sometimes this entails treating the source with larvicides.

When a pesticide application is necessary the technician will use a variety of equiptment, including but not limited to:

  • Backpack sprayer
  • ATV spreader
  • Crop-duster drone
  • Crop-duster airplane

Weber MAD also uses Ultra Low Volume (ULV) fogging machines at night to target adult mosquitoes. Typically a fogging truck is in each municipality at least once a week.

Wide area adulticide applications via airplane are conducted when necessary and only occur over sparsely populated areas.

All adulticide applications, whether by truck or plane, occur after sunset and before sunrise.

Surveillance

Weber MAD deploys more than 30 traps for adult mosquito surveillance, including CO2 baited, Gravid and Rotator types

Traps are placed once a week, for one night. Each trap is sorted by species and counted. Species that are vectors for diseases like West Nile Virus are tested in house via PCR. When West Nile Virus, or any other disease, is detected in a trap the surrounding area is focused in on for adulticide applications, and technician inspections.
